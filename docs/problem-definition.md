# Problem Definition: Capacitated Vehicle Routing Problem

The Capacitated Vehicle Routing Problem (CVRP) is an optimization problem in logistics and transportation. It focuses on finding the best routes for a group of vehicles that must deliver goods from one central depot to several customers.

In the CVRP, each vehicle starts from the depot, visits a number of customers, and returns to the depot after completing its route. Each customer has a demand, for example a required quantity of goods to be delivered.

The word “capacitated” means that every vehicle has a limited carrying capacity. This means a vehicle cannot carry more goods than its maximum allowed load. For example, if a vehicle can carry 100 kg, the total demand of the customers on that vehicle’s route must not be more than 100 kg.

The main objective of the CVRP is to find routes that serve all customers while minimizing the total travel distance, travel time, or transportation cost. At the same time, all vehicle capacity limits must be respected.

The CVRP is important because it helps companies plan deliveries more efficiently. It can reduce fuel costs, save time, improve customer service, and reduce unnecessary travel. This problem is useful in many real-life situations such as parcel delivery, food distribution, waste collection, and supply chain management.
